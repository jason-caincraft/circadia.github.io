The first pass on the backyard line felt close, but still a little nervous when the truck unloaded off the sharper ledges. I wanted the rear to settle sooner without turning the whole chassis into a wallow.

One turn out of the rear preload made the biggest difference right away. The truck still held itself up on the climb, but it stopped hopping as much when the rear axle reached for the next shelf.

Moving the battery forward only a little was enough. The nose tracked better on the uphill transition, and I did not pay much for it on the way back down.

This is probably the setup I will keep for the next session. The next thing worth checking is whether the front tires need a little more support before I start chasing spring changes again.
